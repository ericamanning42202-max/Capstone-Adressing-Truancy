Addressing Truancy Rates in South Carolina Schools

A community-driven capstone project led by Erica D. Manning to identify causes of chronic absenteeism and implement solutions in Cherokee County schools.

This site showcases the research, events, and sign-ups for tutoring programs designed to improve student attendance and success.

Quick Facts

Chronic absenteeism negatively impacts academic performance and graduation rates. This capstone combines local data analysis, community partnerships, and targeted programs to reduce truancy in Cherokee County.

Why this project?

Students who miss school frequently are more likely to fall behind academically and disengage from school. By combining local data analysis, school collaboration, and targeted programs, this project identifies root causes and delivers solutions that keep students in class.

Career Plans

Guidance counselor • Social worker • Criminal data analyst

Bio

Erica combines coursework in psychology and criminal justice with community outreach experience to create evidence-based programs supporting student attendance, family engagement, and academic success.

Project Summary

This capstone project analyzes Cherokee County attendance data, identifies key drivers of truancy, implements a pilot tutoring program in partnership with Kids Club of America, and measures early outcomes to recommend scalable interventions.

Partners: Kids Club of America; Cherokee County School District
